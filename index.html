Buy-Sell Agreements

ESOP's must have business valuations done annually for record keeping purposes as well as every time the ESOP buys or sells stock. To do a business valuation for an ESOP, an appraiser must be well-versed in both government compliance requirements for ESOP valuations and the impacts on value of differing ESOP structures and financing.

SOP 50

Lenders seeking a guarantee for a loan above $350,000 for a transaction involving a change of ownership must get an independent third-party valuation from a qualified source. This is in addition to the longstanding requirement for an independent third-party valuation in the case of a close relationship between the buyer and seller

Estate & Gift Tax

Estate planning has gone from a sporadic, often one-time-only exercise to a dynamic, regularly monitored and updated process. Accordingly, periodic valuations for estate planning (and gifting) purposes are becoming the norm.

Fairness Opinions

Certain equity transactions (such as sales, mergers & acquisitions) may require an opinion regarding whether the transaction is fair from a financial point of view to a shareholder, or group of shareholders. Such an opinion is always required when when one of the shareholders is an ESOP.

Issuance of Stock Options

When a privately-held company decides to issue stock options it needs to have its stock valued so it can set the strike price (also known as the exercise price) of the stock options. It is often advisable to create a buy-sell agreement that clearly establishes the valuation provisions for the stock that will be received upon exercise of the stock options.
